CURRENT RESEARCH

Belle II is the detector of an e+e- collider that will be built at KEK lab in Tuskuba, Japan. My goal is to understand the simulation of beam backgounds (Touschek, beam gas, and radiative Bhabha), and determine whether the simulations match reality. To that end, I will be installing He-3 thermal neutron detectors into the commissioning detector of Belle II, known as the BEAST II. In addition, I study the simulation studies to see what the effect of the beam backgrounds will be on the electromagnetic calorimeter. I am also studying shielding options to mitigate these backgrounds.
